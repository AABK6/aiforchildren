Call for Action: Launching a Multi-Stakeholder Coalition for Beneficial AI in Child Development

This page details our call for action to establish an international coalition dedicated to ensuring that AI supports and safeguards the developmental needs of every child.

Children under 18 represent 25% of our current world population—and 100% of our future. Their unique developmental stage makes them exceptionally receptive to opportunities and equally vulnerable to digital risks. As AI becomes a key part of education, communication, and everyday life, we must ensure that these technologies are designed with children’s well-being at their core.

AI holds enormous promise—from personalized learning and equitable access to education to innovative solutions that support children’s rights. Yet, if unchecked, AI may also amplify risks that hinder healthy development. It is our ethical responsibility to shape a future in which AI is a force for good.

Preamble: Recognizing a Pivotal Moment for Future Generations

During this critical phase, children’s brains are rapidly evolving as they learn, absorb, and interact with the world around them. Their immense capacity for growth makes it imperative that we harness AI’s transformative potential to support—not disrupt—their development.

Guiding Principles for Beneficial AI

  • Child Safety: Prioritize the safety and well-being of every child.
  • Child-Centered AI: Design AI solutions that address children’s developmental needs and respect their rights.
  • Ethical, Equitable, and Inclusive AI: Ensure AI benefits all children, regardless of background or ability.
  • Age-Appropriate Design: Align AI systems with children’s cognitive and socio-emotional stages.
  • Transparency and Fairness: Clearly communicate AI practices and maintain accountability.
  • Family-Inclusive Design: Equip parents and guardians with tools to manage children’s AI experiences.
  • Data Privacy: Protect children’s personal data with robust, transparent safeguards.

Objectives and Actions

  1. Establish Guidelines: Engage all stakeholders in continuous dialogue to develop and refine guidelines that ensure AI products support child development.
  2. Leverage Scientific Evidence: Promote long-term studies to assess AI’s developmental, psychological, and societal impacts on children.
  3. Create a Collaborative Network: Build a diverse network of experts to guide and support the evolution of child-centered AI.
  4. Facilitate Transdisciplinary Collaborations: Foster international partnerships among governments, tech companies, educators, and researchers.
  5. Promote AI Education and Literacy: Develop comprehensive programs and resources that empower stakeholders to use AI safely and responsibly.
